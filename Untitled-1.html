What is Menstruation?

Menstruation, or having a period, is the monthly shedding of the lining of the uterus. It usually starts between ages 9 and 15 and is a normal sign that the body is growing and becoming capable of pregnancy in the future.

Common Signs & Symptoms

Many people experience one or more of the following during their menstrual cycle:

  • Mild to strong abdominal cramps
  • Bloating or feeling heavy
  • Breast tenderness
  • Mood swings or irritability
  • Headaches or feeling tired

If symptoms are very severe, it is important to talk to a health professional.

Good Menstrual Hygiene Practices

  • Change pads or tampons every 4–6 hours or sooner if full.
  • Wash the genital area with clean water (mild, unscented soap if needed).
  • Always wash your hands before and after changing menstrual products.
  • Wear clean, breathable cotton underwear.
  • Wrap used pads or tampons and throw them in a trash bin, not in the toilet.

Do's and Don'ts

Do's

  • Keep a small period kit in your bag (pad, tissue, extra underwear).
  • Track your cycle using a calendar or mobile app.
  • Drink plenty of water and eat nutritious food.
  • Do light exercise or stretching to help relieve cramps.

Don'ts

  • Do not use the same pad or tampon for a very long time.
  • Do not flush pads, tampons, or liners in the toilet.
  • Do not feel ashamed to ask questions or seek help.

When Should I See a Doctor?

Seek medical advice if:

  • You bleed so heavily that you need to change your pad every 1–2 hours.
  • Your period lasts more than 7 days.
  • You have severe pain that stops you from doing normal activities.
  • You have no period for several months and you are not pregnant.
  • You feel dizzy, faint, or notice very large blood clots.
